I feel at home in the world when I am swimming, playing piano and discussing music and literature with my brother. And I love to cook for my wonderful friends!

What has drawn you to coding?

I am generally unsatisfied if I feel I only have a surface level understanding of a process or mechanism. I have a deep desire to understand how things around me function. Whether it's something I feel relates to me immediately - like a biological process, the breaks on my road bike, a new weird sound on a modular synthesiser or the dynamics of historical transition between epochs. I have a keen pleasure for getting to the fundamentals of what drives the processes that occur around me and I always have fun doing it! Coding presents an exciting way for me to help shape the world around me and contribute to something bigger than myself.

What do you hope for from the course?

I hope to feel immersed in a learning community. I've taken up a few hobbies and interests since graduating and having time without work (following a serious spinal injury), but it can be less gratifying without having a reciprocal social and formal environment. Learning a complex new skill like coding or a language requires a consistent learning routine and environment and I feel the full-time schedule will satisfy the immersion I think is so crucial for this kind of learning. The other equally important thing that I am excited by is cultural element involved. I look forward to being around other people who are learning alongside me and learning from educators who are passionate about their craft. I hope to be equipped with the skills I need to be capable of creating programs or components of programs that can concretely contribute something to the goings on of the world - in a professional setting but also a social setting.

SO MUCH! I'm here because I have an amazing opportunity from MYOB to develop a new skill set.
